About Me

Engineer. Problem solver. Currently in Edmonton.

I'm Rayner Dcunha, a Computer Engineering student at the University of Alberta in my third year of a Software Co-Op program. I build things that sit at the crossroads of software architecture, data engineering, and embedded systems.

My most recent work was a Dean's Research Awards Research Assistantship, where I built Python automation tools to analyze knowledge graphs with 5,000+ nodes — applying Neo4j and Cypher to surface structural patterns in complex graph databases.

I thrive in environments where I can learn fast and ship real things. Whether it's designing a recursive algorithm, writing Cypher queries against a large graph, or CAD-ing a physical prototype — I care about the craft at every layer of the stack.

Outside of engineering, I speak English, Hindi, and Konkani. I swim, play badminton, and experiment in the kitchen whenever I get the chance.

Currently open to 4 or 8-month internships starting January 2026. Based in Edmonton, AB. Originally from Muscat, Oman.
